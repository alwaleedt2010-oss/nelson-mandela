  • July 18, 1918
    Birth

    Nelson Mandela was born in the small village of Mvezo, South Africa into the royal Thembu family of the Xhosa-speaking people.

  • September 10, 1944
    Co-founds the African National Congress Youth League (ANCYL)

    Mandelaco-founded the African National Congress Youth League (ANCYL) in 1944. Their goal was to radicalize the struggle, pushing the ANC toward protests, boycotts, strikes, and civil disobedience instead of just polite appeals to government.

  • August 5, 1962
    Arrested, start of 27 years in prison
    Nelson Mandela was arrested for leaving South Africa without permission and for encouraging workers’ strikes. Later, in the Rivonia Trial in 1964, he and other ANCYL leaders were charged with sabotage due to them plotting to overthrow the racist system. Unexpectedly, Mandela was sentenced to life in prison instead of being executed.

  • February 11, 1990
    Released from jail
    In 1989, F.W. de Klerk became South Africa’s president. He knew apartheid was unsustainable and had to end, so he chose to negotiate with Mandela and release him from prison instead of fighting endlessly.

  • October 16, 1993
    Wins the Nobel Peace Prize

    As a result of his efforts to peacfully end apartheid, Mandela won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 alongside F.W. De Klerk.

  • May 10, 1994
    Becomes South Africa’s first Black President

    After receiving the most votes in the first democratic multiracial election in South Africa, Nelson was elected to be preseident. Mandela’s election ended decades of apartheid, the system of racial discrimination enforced by the corrupt government.

  • Dec 5, 2013
    Passes away, remembered as a global symbol of peace

    This marked the end of Mandela's life of non-stop fighting against racial injustice. His life remains a lasting symbol of resilience, moral courage, and the pursuit of equality, inspiring generations worldwide.
